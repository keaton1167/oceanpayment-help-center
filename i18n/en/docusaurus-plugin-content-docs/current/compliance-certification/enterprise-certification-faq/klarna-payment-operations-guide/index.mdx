**Operations Guide for Klarna Payment Method**

## 1. Overview

For customers, a seamless and enjoyable shopping experience is paramount. It not only boosts customer satisfaction but also fosters repeat business and long-term loyalty. To create such an experience and effectively elevate customer satisfaction, merchants can focus on optimizing the following key areas:

- Information on merchants’ websites
- Customer experience
- Dispute
## 2. Information on Merchants’ Websites

Merchants should ensure that the information on their websites is as detailed and easy to understand as possible. The content that should be included is as follows for reference:

- **Clear Terms and Conditions**
- **Clear Refund and Return Policy, includes:**
- Estimated cost
- Return address
- What will happen if the customer doesn't retrieve the item
- Return timeframe
- Items that can/cannot be returned (including the handling process for damaged/faulty items)
- **Easily Accessible Contact Information**
- It is preferable to have two different methods of contact
- Customer service operating hours
- Response timeframe for handling feedback on issues
- **Delivery Information**
- Where are the goods shipped from?
- Average shipping time (The delivery duration may vary due to differences in markets or regions).
- **Introduction on Refund Processing Flow**
- Merchant processing time
- It is recommended to provide Klarna-related information.
- **Charges**
- Why is there a charge?
- Whether a refund is available or not?
- **FAQ Page** 
- It is recommended to include a dedicated "Klarna" section on the FAQ page.
- Include Links to "What is Klarna?" and Klarna's customer service.
- Details on refunds/payments processed via Klarna.
## 3. Customer Experience

### 3.1 Checkout

Customers can view the items in their shopping cart and make any changes to the added items at any time. To better enhance the checkout experience for customers, reduce the volume of customer service inquiries related to checkout issues, and build customer trust, we recommend the following:

- **Ensure that customers' shopping cart lists contain accurate information, including:**
- Image
- Description
- Product’s price
- **Display shipping options, costs, and estimated delivery times to facilitate:**
- Customers' ability to choose the shipping method that best suits their needs.
- Customers' understanding of when they can expect to receive their goods.
- **Clearly define the checkout total and all associated fees.**
### 3.2 Product Delivery

After the customer checks out and the order is captured/activated, payment processing from the customer (based on the payment method they selected) will commence. Capturing orders too early or too late often leads to customer inquiries regarding their order status. To effectively reduce the volume of customer service inquiries about the current status of orders, it is recommended that merchants:

- **Ensure that orders are captured/activated when the product is shipped.**
- **Strive to deliver products within 14 days.**
- **Provide customers with a valid logistics tracking ID.**
### 3.3 Post-Sale Communication

After the customer checks out and the product is delivered, to help customers' understanding of information related to their purchased items, it is recommended that merchants:

- **Provide post-sale communication to customers via email. The content of these emails should include:**
- Order confirmation
- Shipping confirmation
- Shipping details with tracking information and estimated delivery time
- **Provide Klarna with a customer service email address to:**
- Enable Klarna to refer customers to the merchant effectively.
- Allow Klarna to share the merchant's contact information with customers when necessary.
- **If a customer cancels an order or initiates a return, send the customer a notification confirming the cancellation or refund to:**
- Confirm that the order has been canceled/refunded.
- Confirm when the refund will be processed and when it will be initiated.
### 3.4 Customer Service

When issues arise during or after a product purchase, one of the most critical aspects for customers is receiving assistance in a timely manner. Over 90% of customers highly value excellent customer service and base their decision to repurchase on the quality of customer service they receive.

If a customer contacts a merchant with a query but does not receive a response, they may reach out to Klarna for assistance. However, since Klarna cannot answer all customer inquiries, the customer will ultimately need to contact the merchant again for confirmation. This not only incurs additional operational costs for the merchant but also results in a negative customer experience. Therefore, to resolve issues promptly, enhance the customer experience, and improve customer repurchase rates, the best practices recommended for merchants are:

- **Promptly notify customers that their query or feedback has been received and respond to them within two business days.**
- **Ensure that all questions and relevant information in customer inquiries are answered to the best of your ability.**
- **Avoid or minimize the use of auto-replies (as they often fail to address the customer's query).**

## 4. Dispute

A dispute arises when a customer, after purchasing a product, questions whether they are liable for payment.

### 4.1 Dispute Reasons

Regarding the types of disputes, they can be summarized as follows:

- **Returns:** The customer claims to have returned all or part of the order.
- **Goods not received:** The customer claims not to have received all or part of the order.
- **Incorrect Invoice:** The customer claims the received invoice is incorrect (e.g., missing discounts or inaccurate items listed).
- **Faulty Goods:** The customer claims to have received damaged goods or that part of the order is missing.
- **Already Paid:** The customer claims to have already paid the Klarna invoice (e.g., by stating they paid directly to the merchant).
### 4.2 Dispute Processing Flow

- **Initiation**
After a customer raises a dispute, there is a 21-day window to resolve the issue. If no resolution is reached within this timeframe and the customer has provided sufficient evidence to validate the dispute, the dispute will be forwarded to the merchant via Oceanpayment dispute notification. Meanwhile, merchants can search and handle disputes on Oceanpayment Merchant Portal / ODPM platform.

- **Handling**
Through the channels specified in the dispute process, Klarna will share the relevant information collected with the merchant and notify the merchant of the information required to resolve the dispute. This includes providing all information requested in the dispute and/or evidence that supports the merchant's position (e.g., proof of delivery, terms and conditions on the website, etc.).

**Note:** For "Returns" disputes, the 21-day period is calculated from the return date, not the creation date of the dispute.

- **Resolution**
Once Klarna receives feedback from the merchant, a decision will be made to resolve the dispute:

- If the merchant provides sufficient evidence, the customer will be held liable for payment.
- If the dispute is resolved in favor of the customer or if the merchant fails to respond to the dispute, a chargeback will be processed.
### 4.3 Optimized Handling Strategies for Major Customer Complaint Causes

### 4.4 Returns, Cancellations, and Refunds

- **Return**
Failure by merchants to process returns promptly after a customer initiates one can lead to dissatisfaction and even escalate into disputes. Enhancing the customer experience by enabling refunds without requiring prior contact with the merchant for instructions or return labels is recommended. The following solutions are proposed for return processing:

- Provide Self-Service Return Options
Offer customers methods to initiate returns independently (e.g., prepaid return labels, QR code generation for returns).

- Process Returns Within 14 Days
Merchants shall register returned items immediately upon receipt to ensure the customer service team can respond to inquiries within 2 business days and be fully informed about the return details, including:

- Return Process
- Reasons for Return Rejection
- Return Registration Confirmation
- Estimated Refund Timeline
- Provide customers with a local return address to minimize additional shipping costs and reduce refund processing times.
- Notify customers that refunds for in-store returns will be processed via Klarna (or the applicable payment service provider), not directly by the merchant.
- Clear Return Information
Prominently display your return policy and a Return FAQ section on your website. Key details to include:

- Specify which products qualify for returns
- Return Process
- Expected Refund Timeline

- **Cancellations and Refunds**
When customers return items to the designated return address, they expect a prompt refund. Delayed refunds often lead to potential dispute fees and a negative customer experience. Recommended solutions include:

- Issue refunds as soon as possible after receiving the returned item, with a maximum processing time of 3 business days.
- Ensure refunds are returned to the customer’s original payment method。

**Note:**

Refunds must be processed via the original payment method, not directly by the merchant. If the merchant issues a refund directly, Klarna’s invoice will remain valid and must still be paid by the customer, causing confusion and additional operational costs for the merchant.

By registering the return through the merchant’s Klarna portal or the payment provider’s backend, Klarna will adjust the invoice and send an updated version to the customer. If the customer has already paid the invoice, Klarna will initiate the refund.

**When receiving such disputes, providing the following information can help merchants improve their success rate in appealing the dispute:**

Klarna will ask the merchant if they received the return. If the merchant did receive it, please ensure the invoice for the related order is adjusted. If the merchant received the return but does not accept it, make sure to explain the reasoning in the comment section.

Please note that if the merchant selects the option indicating they will make adjustments to the statement, they are committing to issuing a refund equal to the disputed amount. If the merchant does not perform the refund within 96 hours of responding, Klarna will automatically initiate a chargeback for the disputed amount.

### 4.5 Goods not received

In online shopping, providing accurate and transparent information to customers is crucial. This section highlights best practices for merchants, including tracking information shared with customers, efficient order processing, and the importance of effective communication on your website. By prioritizing these areas, you can significantly enhance customer satisfaction, reduce disputes, and improve operational efficiency.

Prioritizing the organization of order and delivery information helps prevent non-receipt of goods, thereby reducing disputes. **We recommend the following best practices:**

- **Fast Delicery**
Ensure items are delivered to customers within 20 days from the order date.

- **Accurate Order Records**
Maintain records at the time of shipment. If multiple packages are sent (partial order fulfillment), record only the quantity of items dispatched.

- **Comprehensive Cart Page**
The cart page is an ideal location for customers to access delivery information, including:

- Delivery options and costs
- Additional shipping or customs fees
- Estimated delivery timeframes
- **Send Confirmation Emails**
Proactively sharing information with customers reduces subsequent issues. The best times to send confirmation emails are:

- After order placement (order confirmation)
- At the time of shipment
- **Provide Tracking Numbers**
Offer tracking numbers to enable customers to independently monitor their order status. Tracking numbers can be shared via:

- Order confirmation emails
- Shipment notification emails
- **Clear Delivery Information**
Send customers easily accessible details for tracking purposes. We recommend clarifying the following in your website’s FAQs:

- Pre-shipment preparation time
- Typical shipping timelines
- Estimated delivery times by country/region
- How to obtain tracking numbers
- **Effective Support**
To enable customers to report quickly, we suggest:

- Providing a feedback process via a dedicated email address or user-friendly form
- Explaining in your FAQs how customers can report issues or initiate returns, including specific steps
- Informing customers in your FAQs when and how to contact Klarna
- **Comprehensive Customer Service**
Ensure your customer service team responds to inquiries within 2 business days and can provide:

- Order and shipping confirmations
- Estimated delivery times/tracking numbers
- Instructions on reporting issues to the merchant
- Guidance on when and how to contact Klarna

**When receiving such disputes, providing the following information can help merchants improve their success rate in appealing the dispute:** 

- **Physical goods**
- The tracking ID of the delivery
- The address to which the order was delivered
- The Date the order was delivered
- The delivery status of the parcel (optional)
- One piece of additional evidence to prove the entire order was delivered. Options include, but are not limited to, packing list or delivery note, weight of items and parcel etc.
- A signature from the customer showing the item was received (for orders >750 EUR) (optional)
- **Intangible Goods**
- The date the order was sent
- The email address the order was sent to (optional)
- The IP the order was sent to (optional)
- Any evidence the item was received, accessed or used (optional)
- **On Demand/Food Delivery**
- The customer's name (optional)
- The date and time of delivery (optional)
- The delivery status of the parcel (optional)
- The address to which the order was delivered (optional)
- The delivery choice (optional)

**Note:** In addition to the requests related to each category, the below information is required for all Goods not received disputes:

- The tracking ID of the delivery
- The shipping carrier used to deliver the package
- The Date the order was delivered
### 4.6 Faulty Goods

Faulty goods can severely undermine the shopping experience and erode customer trust in both the merchant and Klarna. In this section, as a merchant, you play a pivotal role in ensuring product quality and customer satisfaction. Accurate product descriptions, clear definitions of damaged or mismatched items, and straightforward reporting processes empower customers to make informed decisions. Merchants should efficiently address issues by submitting evidence and adhering to return/exchange policies, offering refunds or replacements promptly.

**The following best practices will significantly help reduce customer complaints related to faulty goods:**

- **Accurate Product Descriptions**
Provide detailed and precise product descriptions, including specifications and high-quality images, displayed to customers during shopping and checkout.

- Return process
- Explain valid reasons for rejecting returns
- Confirm return registration
- State estimated refund processing times
- **Define Defective/Non-Conforming Items**
Establish a transparent policy that explicitly defines what constitutes a defective or non-conforming item and explains how the merchant will address such cases.
- **Provide a Reporting Process**
Ensure customers can easily report defective or non-conforming items through:
- Their consumer account dashboard.
- Direct contact with customer service representatives.
- **Resolve Issues Promptly**
Merchants should handle customer requests for defective products swiftly by:
- Issuing refunds.
- Dispatching replacements at the merchant’s expense.
- **Customer Service Support**
Customer service must respond promptly, providing an initial reply within 2 business days to address inquiries about product quality issues, incorrect items received, refund confirmations.

**When receiving such disputes, providing the following information can help merchants improve their success rate in appealing the dispute:** 

- **Add reply (optional)**
- **Attachment (max. 7.3MB)**
**Note:** Questions Klarna commonly ask are "Have you been in contact with the customer?" and "Have you come to an agreement?" (such as refunding the faulty goods or resending the product).

### 4.7 Incorrect Invoice

Transparency is paramount when customers shop online. Merchants must ensure customers are fully aware of the items added to their cart and the total costs they will incur. In cases of upselling, merchants must guarantee that customers understand what is happening and can clearly see the added products and their prices during checkout. If hidden fees are included in the order total or discounts are not explicitly stated, customers are likely to raise concerns with both Klarna and the merchant. This not only causes confusion but may also erode trust in the merchant and Klarna, as customers may feel deceived. To address this, merchants must provide a detailed checkout summary. 

**By adhering to these best practices, partners can reduce operational costs and disputes.**

- **Remove Out-of-Stock Items**
The best approach is to regularly update your website with accurate inventory status. To further assist customers, display estimated restocking dates for out-of-stock items and offer a notification option for when they become available.
- **Ensure Accurate Checkout Information**
Clearly present product details before checkout, including:
- Accurate item name, image, description, size, quantity, and price.
- The final order price after discounts, promotions, and additional fees are applied.
- Recommend supplementary products (upsells) while explicitly allowing customers to skip or remove them.
- **Clarify Subscription Services**
If offering subscriptions, explicitly inform customers before purchase to avoid order cancellations and disputes.
- **Prevent Duplicate Orders**
Implement safety checks during checkout, such as confirmation pop-ups or messages, to verify order success and prevent duplicates.
- **Send Order Confirmation Emails**
Promptly email customers after order completion with:
- Order number.
- Item details (image, description, size, quantity, price).
- The final order price after discounts, promotions, and additional fees.
- If shipped in multiple packages, note that Klarna will generate separate billing entries.
- **Submit Accurate Orders to Klarna**
Send Klarna the same item names, photos, and order details displayed on your website and in confirmation emails to align with customer expectations and prevent disputes.
- **Report Incorrect Invoice Information to Klarna**
Clarify in your website’s FAQ that customers should contact you for incorrect invoices or duplicate orders. Issues to forward to Klarna include:

- Payment dates and due dates.
- How to dispute orders in the Klarna app.
- Refunds (only for registered returns).

- **Comprehensive Customer Service**
Ensure your customer service team responds to inquiries within 2 business days and can address:

- Out-of-stock items.
- Invoice errors.
- Duplicate order issues.
- Necessary actions to resolve problems.
- When to contact you or Klarna.

**When receiving such disputes, providing the following information can help merchants improve their success rate in appealing the dispute:** 

- **Add reply**
- **Attachment (max. 7.3MB)**
**Note:** Klarna will request from the merchant to prove that the invoice created by the merchant is correct.
