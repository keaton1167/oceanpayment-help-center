## 1. Preface:

### 1.1 Overview of ODPM Digital Platform Multi Terminal

The ODPM digital platform caters to the multi terminal usage scenarios of merchants, launching PC, H5 mobile, and WeChat mini program. The PC end focuses on overall business operation management, the H5 mobile end focuses on business information query, the mini program focuses on the financial section, data analysis, and message notification. Overviews of each terminal page, functional modules, and functional buttons are detailed below:

### 3.1 Roles

- Click on 【Operational Setting】-【Roles】to set roles based on the account user's role or department function. After entering the corresponding information and checking the corresponding permissions on the settings page, click 【Submit】 to successfully set the role. Then enter the 【Role】 page, where you can view a list of all the roles set for the current account, and modify the information or permissions for a certain role, or delete a certain role.

### 3.2 Data Permission

The reasons for blocking are generally divided into interface issue and risk management:
- Interface Issue:
Generally, the transaction is blocked because the interface parameters transmitted by the merchant do not meet the rules required by the API document, in this case, the merchant's technical staff is generally required to check the interface parameter settings to fix the problem.
-  Risk Management:
Generally, the transaction triggers a risk control rule configured by Oceanpayment for the merchant to be blocked, in this case, the merchant can verify the corresponding transaction information as needed, such as IP addresses or email addresses from high-risk countries, etc., and pull the corresponding order information into the blacklist.
In addition to interface issue and risk management, there are also other uncommon situations that cannot be judged, and merchants can contact your exclusive operation supporter to consult the specific reasons.
It should be noted that the "traffic source" information is provided, provided that the merchant has passed the value of this parameter to Oceanpayment, so that Oceanpayment can distinguish whether the transaction is from the web or wap/app from this filter condition.

## 11. All Exceptions (PC/H5)

### 11.1 Dispute

When a no-response-required dispute cannot reach an agreeable solution, Klarna gets involved in the dispute process of the order, and this dispute becomes a response-required dispute. Each dispute has a corresponding deadline, the deadline shows the final date until the merchant can respond to the request. For a Klarna order, as long as the dispute remains unresolved, the consumer can continue to seek more information from the merchant and initiate multiple dispute requests. 

For a Klarna dispute order, if the consumer accepts the merchant's proposed solution, the current dispute is considered closed. If a consumer has accepted and closed a Klarna disputed order, but later continues to dispute, they can initiate a new dispute case. Until the new dispute case is resolved, the consumer can still continue to seek further information from the merchant and initiate multiple dispute requests.

If a consumer does not accept the merchant's proposed solution for a Klarna disputed order, the dispute will escalate to a chargeback, and Klarna will directly refund the consumer. 

Click [Administration Center] - [All Exception] - [Dispute]
Merchants can query the corresponding dispute transaction information according to Account Order No. or OP Payment ID. Disputes can also be filtered by Transaction Type, Dispute Type, Process Status, Number of Disputes, Klarna status etc.

Time types that can be selected in the filter: Application Time, Transaction Time, Dispute Time, Dispute response deadline, Close at.
- Application Time: The time when response-required disputes and no-response-required disputes are updated in the back-end;
- Transaction Time: The transaction time of this transaction
- Dispute Time: The time when a no-response-required case occurs or a no-response-required case was escalated to a dispute.
- Dispute response deadline: The end time of the Klarna no-response-required order or the deadline for submitting a Klarna dispute appeal in Oceanpayment back-end.
- Closed at: The closing time of the no-response-required disputes or response-required disputes at Klarna system.

Up to five types of time can be selected simultaneously for searching.

### 11.2 Chargeback

A chargeback is the reversal of financial transaction, typically initiated by a customer (the cardholder), who disputes a sale on his or her credit card. Generally occurring within 180 days after the transaction, some industries and issuers can accept chargeback applications for a longer period of time. When a chargeback occurs, merchants can submit re-presentment before the reply deadline if they do not agree with the chargeback. And if merchants win the re-presentment, fund will back to the merchant's account. Besides, please note that regardless of whether the appeal is successful or not, the bank will charge a chargeback fee.

### 11.3 Retrieval

When cardholders are unsure whether a transaction on the issuer's statement was made by themselves, they usually request a copy of the relevant transaction document from their card issuer to determine whether the transaction was made by them. The above behavior is called retrieval. After the retrieval request occurs, the issuer freezes the transaction amount and waits for further verification before deciding whether to unfreeze it. If the fund of the transaction is frozen, the settlement of this transaction will be postponed.

### 11.4 Fraud

Each issuer will report consumer fraud to the corresponding agency (TC40 or SAFE). Based on the reports issued by these agencies, the acquiring bank and the issuers will understand the fraud risk of the transaction and adjust the risk control strategy timely to ensure that the fraud is eliminated. Transactions are controlled within reasonable limits. Visa's fraud reporting is TC40; MasterCard's fraud reporting is SAFE. We call the transactions covered by these reports Fraud. Failure to respond to the fraud notification may result in an 85% probability of a chargeback. When a chargeback is initiated, the bank will comply with the chargeback process, and will charge an additional chargeback processing fee apart from the deducted transaction amount, and will assess the chargeback rate as well.
